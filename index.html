    Recipe

    • Season both sides of the beef with salt, pepper and flour. Sear in a pan with butter and set aside.
    • Slice the onion in thin half rings and add to the same pan. Fry down for 5-10 minutes.
    • Add tomato paste, red wine vinegar, bay leaves, cloves, beef stock and salt and pepper to taste.
    • Bring to a light simmer and add the beef back in, cover and let simmer for at least 3-4 hours or until it falls apart (the longer the better).
    • When the beef is almost done, stir the ontbijtkoek through it. This will make the sauce thick.
    • Serve with the mash, pickled cabbage(or something else fresh), any vegetables you like and top with some fresh parsley.

    Recipe

    • Cut all the vegetables into bite-sized pieces, not too small.
    • Cut the beef into bite-sized pieces, season with salt and pepper and cover with flour.
    • Heat a pan with butter and oil and sear the beef in batches until browned on all sides.
    • Set aside and add the bacon to the pan, fry until crispy.
    • Add the vegetables to the pan and fry until softened.
    • Deglaze the pan with the wine and stock
    • Add the beef back in and add the thyme and bay leaves.
    • Let simmer for at least 3 hours or until the beef is tender.
    • Season to taste and serve with some fresh parsley and a side, for example a mash

    Recipe

    Dough

    • Bloom the yeast by combining a bit of water with sugar and putting the yeast in it to see if it is alive.
    • Add the rest of the water, olive oil and salt to the mixture
    • Add the flour and mix well, if it stays too sticky, add more flour
    • Knead long enough so it passed the "windows test"(can be stretched thin enough so the dough becomes transparant)
    • Add to oiled up containers(preferably seperate) to let it rise. +-2 hours on room temperature, or in the fridge for 1-7 days
    • When risen, make pizza shape out of the dough, use flour for convenience.
    • Top with the sauce and any toppings you like
    • Bake in a preheated oven at the highest possible heat, check often to see if it is ready (7? minutes)
